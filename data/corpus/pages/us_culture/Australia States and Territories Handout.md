# Australia: States and Territories

Australia is composed of six states, two major mainland territories and eight offshore territories (e.g., Norfolk Island, Christmas Island, etc.). The powers of the states are greater than the powers of the territories.

These are the six Australian states:

**New South Wales** is located on Australia's east coast, north of Victoria and south of Queensland. Its capital, Sydney, is the oldest European settlement in Australia. The two architectural landmarks of Sydney are the Harbour Bridge and the Opera House. The city hosted the 2000 Olympic Games.

**Queensland** covers the north-eastern part of Australia, its northern part reaching far into the tropics. In the north, it is separated from Papua New Guinea by the Torres Strait, in the south it borders New South Wales. The Great Barrier Reef, parallel to the coast of Queensland, is the largest living organism in the world. The capital city of Queensland is Brisbane.

**South Australia**, located in the central part of southern Australia, is the country's wine growing region. Its capital city is Adelaide.

**Tasmania** is an island located south of Victoria. It is separated from the continent by the Bass Strait. Tasmania is the smallest state in Australia. It is well-known for its farming, forestry and tourism. The capital city is Hobart.

**Victoria**, located in the south eastern corner of Australia is the smallest mainland state, but it is the most densely populated one. Its capital city, Melbourne, formerly the national capital, developed as a result of Victorian gold rushes.

**Western Australia** covers the western part of the Australian continent. It is very large in size (about the size of Western Europe), but sparsely populated. Most of the inhabitants live in Perth, its capital, or the surrounding areas. The state has an important role in Australia's trade with Asia.

The two major mainland territories and one of the offshore territories (Norfolk Island) have been granted a limited right of self-government. The two mainland territories are the Northern Territory and the Australian Capital Territory.

**Northern Territory** is a large territory in north central Australia. Because of its arid and semi- arid climate, much of it is covered by desert, while the northern part has a tropical climate, characterised by high levels of humidity. It is often affected by tropical cyclones in the wet season. Its capital city is Darwin.

**Australian Capital Territory** is a very small, densely populated territory located entirely within the boundaries of New South Wales. It was established in the early 20<sup>th</sup> century as a neutral territory to be the location of the nation's capital, Canberra, before the city was built.
