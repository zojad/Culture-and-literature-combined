# Canada: Provinces and Territories

Canada is composed of ten provinces and three territories. The powers of the provinces are greater than the powers of the territories.

These are the ten Canadian provinces:

**Alberta** (Capital: Edmonton) is the westernmost of Canada's three Prairie Provinces. Its western border with British Columbia is formed by the peaks of the Rocky Mountains. The province is rich in timber, and natural resources such as oil, gas and coal.

**British Columbia** (Capital: Victoria) is the westernmost province of Canada, located on the Pacific Coast. In the last fifty years it has become one of the leading provinces in terms of economic growth and opportunities. Vancouver is Canada's largest port on its West Coast.

**Manitoba** (Capital: Winnipeg) is the easternmost of Canada's three Prairie Provinces. Agriculture is important in the southern part of the province, while the northern part is covered by forests.

**New Brunswick** (Capital: Fredericton) is one of the Maritime Provinces, although its climate is more continental than the climate of the other Maritime Provinces. While the majority of the population is English speaking, New Brunswick has a significant French-speaking minority (approximately 35% of the population).

**Newfoundland and Labrador** (Capital: St. John's) is the easternmost province of Canada. It comprises two parts: the island of Newfoundland and the Labrador Peninsula. The climate ranges from polar to subarctic, with parts of Newfoundland having humid continental climate.

**Nova Scotia** (Capital: Halifax) is a small province located in southeast Canada. It is one of the Maritime Provinces. Traditionally, fishing was a major economic activity in the province; however, in recent decades other sectors (small businesses, tourism, etc.) have grown in importance.

**Ontario** (Capital: Toronto), a province in south central Canada, is the second largest province in the country in terms of land area and the largest province in terms of population. Canada's capital, Ottawa is located in Ontario; however, the provincial capital is Toronto, the largest city in the country and the heart of the country's financial sector.

**Prince Edward Island** (Capital: Charlottetown) is the smallest province in Canada. It is one of the Maritime Provinces. Its economy relies on agriculture, fishing and tourism.

**Quebec** (Capital: Quebec City) is located in central Canada. It is the largest province in the country. Most of its population is French-speaking and it is the only Canadian province with French as the only official language. The largest city in the province is Montreal, the second largest city in Canada.

**Saskatchewan** (Capital: Regina) is one of the Prairie Provinces, located east of Alberta and west of Manitoba. Its climate ranges from subarctic in the north to continental in the south. It is one Canada's agricultural provinces.

These are the three Canadian territories:

**The Northwest Territories** (Capital: Yellowknife) is located in northern Canada, east of Yukon and west of Nunavut. The Northwest Territories is sparsely populated with the First Nations constituting over one third of the population. It is very rich in natural resources, most notably, gold, petroleum and natural gas.

**Nunavut** (Capital: Iqaluit) was established as recently as 1999. Prior to 1999 it was part of the Northwest Territories. Nunavut, located east of the Northwest Territories, is the largest of the three territories. It encompasses a significant part of northern Canada and the Arctic Archipelago. It is the most sparsely populated part of Canada with the Inuit constituting the majority of the population (approximately three quarters).

**Yukon** (Capital: Whitehorse) is located in northwest Canada, east of the Northwest Territories and west of Alaska. As the other two territories, Yukon is also quite sparsely populated. Due to its natural resources, the economy in the region has traditionally relied on mining. Today, tourism is an important economic activity in the region, relying above all on various outdoor recreational activities, such as angling, hiking, climbing, etc.
