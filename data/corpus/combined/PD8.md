South Asia

The Indian Subcontinent

Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka

Bangladesh, India, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka: former British dependencies

Bhutan and Nepal: some degree of British control over their territories

India

The greater part of the Indian Subcontinent

The second most populous country in the world today

Old civilisations

World religions

India

Early attempts to colonise parts of India (Portuguese, Dutch, French and

British)

Goal: to control the trade in the region

Strategic importance of India (its exports, such as spices and textiles)

British influence: through the British East India Company

British East India Company

Granted a royal charter giving it a monopoly in trade by Elizabeth I in 1600

Originally a commercial company

Gradually became a powerful political force which ruled India

An unsuccessful attempt to rebel against the British East India Company in 1857 (the First War of Indian Independence /the Indian Mutiny)

British Empire - independence

The British Empire seized control of most of the territory

First half of the 20th century a non-violent struggle for independence

Mahatma Gandhi

Independent from the UK in 1947

A republic in 1950

India today

Democracy

Multicultural

Sectarian violence

Border disputes

Poverty

Significant economic growth

Rapidly developing IT industry

The linguistic situation in India

- Very complex
- Over a fifteen hundred languages used by different ethnic groups
- 23 languages are official
- All the official languages have equal status
- Hindi and English are the main languages of politics
- English is the language of the educated classes

Pakistan and Bangladesh

1947: the Partition of British India

Two states: India, with a Hindu majority, and Pakistan, with a Muslim

majority

Violence, riots, forced migrations and even wars

Ongoing conflict between India and Pakistan over issues such as Kashmir

Pakistan and Bangladesh

Until 1971, two regions:

- West Pakistan (present-day Pakistan)
- East Pakistan (present-day Bangladesh)

Geographically separated by India

Pakistan and Bangladesh

- Insurmountable internal economic and political differences
- Escalation of tensions
- 1971: Pakistan Civil War (the Pakistani term) or the Bangladesh Liberation War

(the term used in Bangladesh)

Pakistan

Among the most populous countries in the world

Developing country

Recently experienced some growth in the economy

Two official languages, Urdu and English

Bangladesh

Among the most densely populated countries in the world

One of the poorest counties in the world

Yearly monsoon floods

The official language is Bengali

English is the second language, often used in politics and higher education

Sri Lanka

An island nation in the Indian Ocean off the coast of India

British East India Company seized control of the island (Ceylon) in 1796

British colony since 1802

Gradual transition to autonomy

First free elections were held in 1983

Sri Lanka

Multiethnic

Conflicts between its ethnic groups, especially the Tamils and the Sinhalese

Two official languages in Sri Lanka, Sinhala and Tamil

English is widely spoken on the island
