# Southeast Asia

North of Australia, east of India, south of China

Anglosphere:

- Hong Kong
- Singapore
- Malaysia
- The Philippines

Hong Kong

- Located on the south-eastern coast of China
- Today: a special administrative region of the People's Republic of China
- The last important British colony in Asia
- Returned to China on July 1, 1997
- "One country, two systems" policy for the next 5o years
- Retained a high degree of autonomy and its own legal system

# Hong Kong

- Originally an important port used in international trade with China
- Prominent international trade and financial centre
- Blend of Eastern and Western cultures
- Over 95% of its population is Cantonese (Chinese)

# Languages in Hong Kong

- Cantonese (spoken by the majority at home and in public life)
- Growing importance of Mandarin Chinese since 1997
- Other Chinese languages
- English (the language of international business and science)
- Official languages: English and Chinese (both Cantonese and Mandarin)

# Singapore

- A city-state located on an island on the southern tip of the Malay Peninsula
- 1867 - 1963: a British colony
- 1963 - 1965: part of the Federation of Malaysia
- 1965: independence
- Strategic location: one of Asia's largest ports
- An important international trade and industry centre

# Singapore

One of the most densely populated countries in the world Multi-cultural city

Four official languages:

- Malay (the national language for historical reasons),
- English (the language of trade and politics),
- Mandarin Chinese (the language of the largest ethnic groups, constituting three quarters of the population),
- Tamil (the language of the largest of the Indian ethnic groups)

Singlish (formally known as Singapore Colloquial English)

# The Philippines

- An archipelago located in Southeast Asia
- Between the Philippine Sea and the South China Sea
- A Spanish colony for over 3oo years
- An attempt to gain independence in the 189os: under US control
- Independent country in 1946

# The Philippines
