**SOCIETIES AND CULTURES OF THE ENGLISH- SPEAKING WORLD**

Australia

General information

Southern Hemisphere

World's sixth largest country

Australian mainland, Tasmania and a number of islands in the Indian and Pacific Oceans

Australian mainland is the smallest continent in the world (the Australian Continent)

Climate

The driest and flattest continent on Earth

Largest part of Australia (its central part) is desert or semi-arid grassland

(steppe)

Climate in the northern part of Australia is tropical, in part covered by rainforest

Temperate, oceanic climate is only found in southeastern Australia

Biodiversity

Isolation of the continent

Variety of climate and landscape

Unique ecosystems, great biodiversity

Endemic species

Marsupials: koalas, wombats, possums, kangaroos, etc.

Birds, e.g., the flightless emu, various cockatoo species, etc.

Biodiversity

Feral animals: the Dingo, foxes and rabbits, rodents

Many non-indigenous species have very few or no predators

A threat to many ecosystems and indigenous species

A number of indigenous species endangered or even extinct

History

- First inhabitants reached the continent over 4o ooo years ago
- First Europeans arrived in the 17th century
- 16o6, Willem Jansz or Janszoon
- 177o, James Cook mapped the east coast of Australia and claimed the territory for

the British Crown

History

- 1788, First permanent European settlement: The penal colony of New South Wales
- Other colonies: Tasmania (originally named Van Diemen's Land), South Australia, Victoria, Queensland, etc.
- Not all the colonies were penal colonies (e.g., South Australia)
- 1868, Convict transportation ends

History

- 185os, Discovery of gold
- 19o1, Establishment of a federation of the six colonies: Commonwealth of Australia (Dominion of the British Empire)
- 1915-16, World War I; Defeat at Gallipoli: the birth of the nation (1st major military

action)

- 1931, Statute of Westminster ends most constitutional links between UK and Australia
- 1942, Australia adopts the Statute of Westminster
- First half of the 2oth century: White Australia policy
- 197os: White Australia policy is abandoned

People

Indigenous Australians: Aboriginals and Torres Strait Islanders

European immigrant and descendants of European immigrants

British or Irish descent, but also from Italy, Germany, Greece, the Netherlands, Poland, etc.

Australians of non-European descent: Asian (e.g., Chinese, Filipino, Indian)

Language and culture

Australia has no official language

English is the first language of the majority of the population, used in all spheres of life

A number of languages other than English used by the ethnic minority groups

Australian culture is based on the culture(s) of the British Isles

Multicultural

Aboriginal people

- Diverse in terms of culture and language
- Over 2oo Aboriginal languages on the arrival of the first Europeans
- Today 7o Aboriginal languages; many on the verge of death
- Rich religious and spiritual traditions
- Unique music and art
- History and knowledge were transmitted orally

Aboriginal people

- Aboriginal population declined dramatically after the colonies were established
- Stolen Generations
- Approximately one quarter of Aboriginal people live in remote locations
- The rest live in urban areas and the countryside
- Affected by poverty and unemployment

States and Territories

- Six states (former British colonies) - have more powers than the territories
- Two major mainland territories - a limited right of self-government
- Eight offshore territories (e.g., Norfolk Island, Christmas Island, etc.)

Each state has its own constitution and a state government, divided into the same

three branches as the federal government (legislative, executive, judicial).

Queensland

- Covers the north-eastern part of Australia
- Northern parts reach far into the tropics
- Separated from Papua New Guinea by the Torres Strait
- In the south it borders New South Wales
- Great Barrier Reef, parallel to the coast of Queensland, is the largest living organism in the world
- Capital city: Brisbane

New South Wales

- Australia's east coast
- North of Victoria and south of Queensland
- Capital, Sydney: the oldest European settlement in Australia
- Architectural landmarks of Sydney: Harbour Bridge and the Opera House
- Hosted the 2ooo Olympic Games

Victoria

- South-eastern corner of Australia
- Smallest mainland state
- Most densely populated
- Capital city: Melbourne
- Melbourne was formerly the national capital, and it developed as a result of Victorian gold rushes.

Tasmania

- Island located south of Victoria
- Separated from the continent by the Bass Strait
- Smallest state
- Farming, forestry, wilderness, yachting and tourism
- Capital city: Hobart

South Australia

- Central part of southern Australia
- Country's wine growing region
- Capital city: Adelaide

Western Australia

- Western part of the Australian continent
- Very large, but sparsely populated
- Most inhabitants live in Perth (the capital)
- Important role in Australia's trade with Asia

Northern Territory

- Large territory in north central Australia
- Arid and semi-arid climate: desert
- Northern part: tropical climate
- Often affected by tropical cyclones in the wet season
- Capital city: Darwin

Australian Capital Territory

- Very small, densely populated territory
- Located entirely within the boundaries of New South Wales
- Established in the early 2oth century
- A neutral territory
- The nation's capital, Canberra
