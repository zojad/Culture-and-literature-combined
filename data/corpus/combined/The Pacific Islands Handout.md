# The Pacific Islands: Regions of Oceania

**Melanesia** lies northeast of Australia and south of the Equator. The most important English- speaking areas in Melanesia include the following islands:

- **Fiji** and the **Solomon Islands** (former British colonies),
- **Papua New Guinea** (an Australian mandate after World War I; southern past a former British colony),
- **Vanuatu**, (formerly known as New Hebrides, a former Anglo-French condominium,).

Most of **Micronesian** islands lie north of the Equator in the West Pacific. The most important English-speaking areas in Micronesia include the following islands:

- the **Northern Mariana Islands** and **Guam** (both US territories),
- the **Federated States of Micronesia, Palau** and the **Marshall Islands** (former US territories),
- **Kiribati** (formerly a UK protectorate known as the Gilbert Islands),
- **Nauru** (a former Australian mandate).

**Polynesia** lies in the east-central Pacific Ocean. The most important English-speaking areas in Polynesia include the following islands:

- **American Samoa** (a US territory),
- **Tokelau** (formerly the Union Islands; a New Zealand territory),
- the **Pitcairn Islands** (the only remaining British colony in the Pacific),
- **Tonga** (formerly a British protectorate),
- **Tuvalu**, (formerly the British colony of Ellice Islands),
- **Samoa** (formerly Western Samoa, a former New Zealand mandate),
- the **Cook Islands** and **Niue** (both self-governing, but in free association with New Zealand).

In all of these islands, English is one of the official languages or the official language, although various indigenous languages are used as first languages.
