# UK History Quick Timeline

Use this for fast recall in oral exam answers. Dates and labels are aligned to the corpus wording.

## Early and Medieval Foundations

- `c. 410`: Germanic (Anglo-Saxon) invasions begin.
- `c. 500-820`: Heptarchy (seven Anglo-Saxon kingdoms) develops.
- `597`: St Augustine preaches Christianity to Anglo-Saxons; Canterbury becomes an ecclesiastical center.
- `789-95`: Viking raids begin.
- `954-59`: Anglo-Saxon consolidation into the Kingdom of England.
- `1066`: Harold defeats Norwegians at Stamford Bridge; William I defeats Harold at Hastings; Norman Conquest.
- `1086`: Domesday Book compiled (land/tax survey).
- `1215`: Magna Carta sealed by King John at Runnymede.
- `1265`: Simon de Montfort's proto-parliament.
- `1295`: Model Parliament.

## Renaissance and Early Modern Britain

- `1509`: Accession of Henry VIII.
- `1534-40`: Break with Rome; English Reformation begins.
- `1558-1603`: Elizabeth I; Protestant settlement reconfirmed.
- `1588`: Defeat of the Spanish Armada.
- `1603`: Union of the Crowns (James VI of Scotland becomes James I of England).

## Civil War, Protectorate, Restoration

- `1642-48`: English Civil Wars (King vs Parliament).
- `1649`: Charles I executed; monarchy abolished.
- `1653-58`: Cromwell as Lord Protector (Protectorate/Commonwealth rule).
- `1660`: Restoration under Charles II.
- `1688`: Glorious Revolution (William III and Mary II).
- `1689`: Bill of Rights context (parliamentary sovereignty trajectory).

## Union, Reform, Victorian Era, Empire/Commonwealth

- `1707`: Act/Union of England and Scotland (Great Britain).
- `1801`: Union with Ireland (United Kingdom).
- `1807`: Slave trade abolished in British Empire.
- `1832`: First Reform Act (male franchise expansion).
- `1833`: Slavery abolished in the Empire.
- `1837-1901`: Reign of Queen Victoria.
- `1910-36`: British Empire reaches territorial peak.
- `1921-22`: Irish Free State established; Northern Ireland remains in UK.
- `1931`: British Commonwealth of Nations emerges.

## Quick Exam Links

- Anglo-Saxon and Norman anchors: `1066`, `1086`.
- Constitutional anchors: `1215`, `1265`, `1295`, `1688`.
- Civil War block: `1642-60`.
- Victorian anchor: `1837-1901`.
- Empire/Commonwealth shift: `1910-36` to `1931`.
