# UK and US Literature Quick Timeline

Period-organized exam sheet with authors and works drawn from the corpus work index.

## UK and Anglophone Traditions

### Old English / Anglo-Saxon

- Anonymous Old English poet: *Beowulf*

### Middle English / Medieval

- Geoffrey Chaucer: *The Canterbury Tales*

### English Renaissance (Elizabethan / Jacobean)

- Christopher Marlowe: *Doctor Faustus*
- William Shakespeare: *Romeo and Juliet*, *The Tempest*

### 17th Century: Puritan / Restoration

- John Milton: *Paradise Lost*

### Augustan / Neoclassical / 18th-Century Novel

- Jonathan Swift: *Gulliver's Travels*
- Daniel Defoe: *Robinson Crusoe*
- Samuel Richardson: *Pamela; or, Virtue Rewarded*
- Henry Fielding: *The History of Tom Jones, a Foundling*

### Romantic and Regency

- Jane Austen: *Pride and Prejudice*

### Victorian and Late Victorian

- Charlotte Brontë: *Jane Eyre*
- Emily Brontë: *Wuthering Heights*
- Charles Dickens: *Bleak House*, *David Copperfield*, *Hard Times*, *Great Expectations*
- George Eliot: *Middlemarch*
- Oscar Wilde: *The Importance of Being Earnest*, *The Picture of Dorian Gray*

### Modernism (UK/Irish and related)

- T. S. Eliot: *The Waste Land*, *The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock*
- James Joyce: *Dubliners*, *A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man*, *Ulysses*
- Katherine Mansfield: *Bliss*, *The Garden Party*, *Daughters of the Late Colonel*
- E. M. Forster: *A Passage to India*

### Post-war / Dystopian / Theatre of the Absurd

- George Orwell: *Animal Farm*, *Nineteen Eighty-Four*
- Samuel Beckett: *Waiting for Godot*

### Postcolonial and Global English (Africa, Caribbean, South Africa, South Asia)

- Chinua Achebe: *Things Fall Apart*, *No Longer at Ease*, *Arrow of God*
- Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o: *Weep Not, Child*, *A Grain of Wheat*, *Petals of Blood*
- Wole Soyinka: *The Interpreters*, *Death and the King's Horseman*
- Okot p'Bitek: *Song of Lawino*
- Ben Okri: *The Famished Road*
- Jean Rhys: *Wide Sargasso Sea*
- Sam Selvon: *The Lonely Londoners*
- V. S. Naipaul: *A House for Mr Biswas*, *A Bend in the River*, *In a Free State*
- Salman Rushdie: *Midnight's Children*, *The Satanic Verses*, *The Moor's Last Sigh*
- J. M. Coetzee: *Waiting for the Barbarians*, *Life & Times of Michael K*, *Foe*, *Disgrace*
- Nadine Gordimer: *Burger's Daughter*, *July's People*, *The Conservationist*
- Doris Lessing: *The Grass Is Singing*, *The Golden Notebook*
- Buchi Emecheta: *The Joys of Motherhood*, *Second-Class Citizen*

### Contemporary Canadian (in corpus mapping)

- Alice Munro: *Lives of Girls and Women*, *Who Do You Think You Are?*

## US Literature Track

### American Enlightenment / Revolutionary Era

- Thomas Jefferson: *United States Declaration of Independence*, *Notes on the State of Virginia*
- Thomas Paine: *Common Sense*

### Early American Romanticism / Gothic

- Washington Irving: *Rip Van Winkle*, *The Legend of Sleepy Hollow*
- Edgar Allan Poe: *The Raven*, *The Fall of the House of Usher*, *The Tell-Tale Heart*

### American Renaissance / Transcendentalism / Dark Romanticism

- Henry David Thoreau: *Walden*
- Nathaniel Hawthorne: *The Scarlet Letter*
- Herman Melville: *Moby-Dick*, *Bartleby, the Scrivener*, *Benito Cereno*
- Walt Whitman: *Leaves of Grass*, *Song of Myself*, *When I Heard the Learn'd Astronomer*

### Realism / Regionalism (Late 19th Century)

- Mark Twain: *The Adventures of Tom Sawyer*, *Adventures of Huckleberry Finn*

### Modernism / Jazz Age / Lost Generation / Southern Gothic

- F. Scott Fitzgerald: *The Great Gatsby*, *This Side of Paradise*
- Ernest Hemingway: *The Sun Also Rises*, *A Farewell to Arms*, *The Snows of Kilimanjaro*
- William Faulkner: *The Sound and the Fury*, *As I Lay Dying*, *Absalom, Absalom!*

### Great Depression / Proletarian and Social Realism

- John Steinbeck: *The Grapes of Wrath*, *Of Mice and Men*
- Richard Wright: *Native Son*

### Post-war American Drama

- Eugene O'Neill: *Beyond the Horizon*, *Long Day's Journey into Night*
- Arthur Miller: *Death of a Salesman*, *The Crucible*
- Tennessee Williams: *The Glass Menagerie*, *A Streetcar Named Desire*, *Cat on a Hot Tin Roof*

### Contemporary African American Literature

- Toni Morrison: *The Bluest Eye*, *Sula*, *Song of Solomon*, *Beloved*

## Quick Oral-Exam Packs (3 representatives each)

- Medieval: Chaucer, anonymous *Beowulf* poet, (bridge to Renaissance via early drama context).
- Renaissance: Shakespeare, Marlowe, Milton (late-17th bridge).
- Victorian: Dickens, Brontë sisters, George Eliot / Oscar Wilde.
- US 19th c.: Hawthorne, Melville, Whitman / Twain.
- US modernism: Fitzgerald, Hemingway, Faulkner.
- Postcolonial: Achebe, Ngũgĩ, Rushdie / Coetzee.
