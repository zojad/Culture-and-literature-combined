# Question
Comment on the concept of the British Empire, identify major colonies, and explain the transition to the Commonwealth.

# Thesis
The British Empire expanded through long-term colonial rule and later shifted into a looser Commonwealth structure after decolonization.

# Core points
British Civilization materials describe expansion from early modern settlement, trade, and military power into North America, the Caribbean, Africa, and Asia, with peak global scale in the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. This is important because it frames the Empire as a system, not a single event.

The same corpus links imperial decline to twentieth-century decolonization and shows that former colonies became independent while still maintaining selected institutional ties. This matters because exam answers should separate political sovereignty from historical continuity.

Commonwealth-oriented texts define the Commonwealth as a voluntary association of independent states rather than an imperial hierarchy. This distinction is central for comparing imperial governance with post-imperial cooperation.

# Mini-analysis
A strong answer should move in sequence: imperial growth, political decline, then post-imperial network. If those phases are blended together, the explanation becomes descriptive but not analytical.
