# Question
Name at least one Anglo-Saxon and one Norman monarch and comment on their significance.

# Thesis
Monarchs in these two periods are best compared by their role in territorial control and institutional consolidation.

# Core points
For Anglo-Saxon context, pre-conquest English monarchy is presented within fragmented early medieval political structures and recurrent external pressures. This matters because monarchy operated before later centralized forms.

For Norman context, William I is tied to conquest-era restructuring, including land control and survey practices associated with Domesday Book. This matters because Norman monarchy deepened administrative reach.

Later historical framing treats these transitions as foundational for English/British institutional development. This means monarch examples should be tied to state effects, not only biography.

# Mini-analysis
In exam answers, names score less than causal linkage; the strongest response explains how each monarch changed governance capacity.
