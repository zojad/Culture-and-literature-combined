# Question
Explain what Magna Carta and the Domesday Book represent and why they are significant.

# Thesis
Domesday Book represents early Norman state capacity in taxation/recording, while Magna Carta represents constraints on monarchy that later gained constitutional meaning.

# Core points
The early medieval timeline identifies Domesday Book (1086) as a systematic survey of land and taxable resources in Norman England. This matters because it shows administrative consolidation after conquest.

The same corpus identifies Magna Carta (1215) in the context of baronial pressure on King John and links it to limits on arbitrary royal power. This matters because later constitutional narratives treat Magna Carta as a foundational reference point.

Later constitutional sections show Magna Carta being re-used in political language beyond its immediate feudal context. This helps distinguish original purpose from later symbolic role.

# Mini-analysis
A high-quality answer compares function: Domesday is administrative-fiscal; Magna Carta is constitutional-political. Their significance is different but complementary for state formation history.
