# Question
Explain colonialism and postcolonialism and comment on their role in anglophone literary/cultural contexts.

# Thesis
In the corpus, colonialism is treated as imperial control structure, while postcolonial framing focuses on cultural and political afterlives of that structure.

# Core points
Postcolonial literature materials contrast imperial/colonial systems and trace how colonial expansion shaped language and literary production across regions. This matters because the concept is both political and textual.

Contemporary culture texts connect colonial legacy to ongoing constitutional and symbolic debates in former empire-linked states. This matters because postcolonial analysis includes present-day institutions, not only past conquest.

For exam answers, the strongest approach is conceptual definition followed by one historical and one contemporary example. This avoids abstract jargon without evidence.

# Mini-analysis
A useful distinction is temporal: colonialism names the structure of rule; postcolonialism analyzes the persistence, revision, and contestation of that legacy.
