# Question
Explain the concept of three branches of power in the USA and name key institutions.

# Thesis
US constitutional structure is presented as a separation-of-powers model dividing authority among legislative, executive, and judicial branches.

# Core points
American civilization materials explicitly describe separation of powers as a core constitutional principle and connect it to inter-branch balance. This matters because institutional design is explained through constraint, not only representation.

Constitutional passages and overview materials identify Congress as the legislative center and place executive/judicial authority in distinct institutions. This matters because branch clarity is fundamental in exam responses.

A high-quality answer should move from principle to institutions to political effect (checks, cooperation, and conflict). Without this progression, the response remains descriptive.

# Mini-analysis
For oral use, avoid listing branch names only; add one sentence explaining why divided authority was designed into the system.
